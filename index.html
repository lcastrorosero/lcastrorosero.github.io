From chemical engineering
to the open sea

I'm a researcher with a PhD in Marine Sciences (Universitat de Barcelona / Universitat Politècnica de Catalunya, awarded Excellent Cum Laude), a background in Chemical Engineering, and a Master's in Oceanography and Marine Environment Management.

My work sits where physical oceanography meets environmental risk: hydrodynamic models (ROMS, MOHID), Lagrangian simulations (OceanParcels, LOCATE, GNOME) and satellite & in-situ data analysis, applied to marine litter transport, oil spill forecasting and operational oceanography.

I've worked across Colombia, Spain and the EU — contributing to national hazard-forecasting systems, EU H2020 research, and open-access geospatial platforms — always in close collaboration with international teams and government institutions.

Research highlights

PhD · Black Sea

Floating marine litter: transport & accumulation

Multi-year numerical modelling of where plastic goes in the Black Sea — sources, pathways and accumulation hotspots — combining circulation models, Lagrangian tracking and observations. Dissertation awarded Excellent Cum Laude.

LOCATE · OceanParcels · CMEMS · Sentinel-2
EU H2020 · DOORS

Marine litter modelling in the Black Sea ecosystem

Modelling researcher within the marine litter branch of the EU-funded DOORS project — including backtracking simulations to trace the sources of floating plastic in Romanian waters, published in Science of The Total Environment.

Backtracking · River inputs · International consortium

Developed operational spill-forecasting modules for the Colombian Caribbean and Pacific — including local systems for Cartagena, Santa Marta and Puerto Bolívar — used in environmental emergency response and prevention.
